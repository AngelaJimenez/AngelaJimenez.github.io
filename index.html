RescueCraft: Collaborative VR Environment for Emergency Training

This project involved developing an environment editor specifically for desktop use to create customizable fire evacuation training scenarios. The editor allows trainers to modify and interact with objects, creating realistic environments for diverse training needs.

The focus was on building the logic for object creation and manipulation, providing an effective and engaging way for users to learn and practice fire evacuation procedures. This interactive approach ensures that trainees can adapt to various situations in a safe, virtual space.

While the current version is designed for desktop use, the project is envisioned to support future collaboration between VR and desktop users. This will enhance its application and make training sessions more versatile and inclusive.

The editor's flexibility allows trainers to create scenarios that reflect real-world challenges, helping firefighters and emergency responders to develop crucial skills. This hands-on approach is crucial for mastering optimization techniques and safety protocols.

  • Oculus, XROrigin.

  • Logic development for interactive environments

Teaching Geometry with VR
This project aimed to analyze the impact of virtual reality on children's learning of geometry, focusing on students aged 9 to 14. Collaborating with a partner, I developed a Unity environment that we imported into Altspace, a VR platform known for its educational and social interaction capabilities. The project involved two main components: first, students observed geometric figures alongside their corresponding equations, allowing them to visualize different transformations. The second part was more interactive, with exercises that required students to identify transformations between figures, encouraging active learning and engagement. Feedback from the six participants revealed that VR had a positive effect on their understanding of geometric concepts. This outcome suggests that Altspace is an effective tool for educational purposes, particularly in enhancing both learning and social participation.
FreestAIle
The purpose of this research project was to analyze the limits of the Artificial Intelligence branch specialized in Natural Language Processing (No! this is not a Visual Computing project, this is one I made on AI in order to explore other fields that some day may work on VR). As a result, the idea of this project was to design an algorithm capable of creating a written production made of sentences that follow a logical syntax scheme and a semantics defined by the meaning of each word that is chosen. This algorithm seeks to imitate the process carried out by people who perform the art of freestyle, an activity that consists in producing a type of lyricism characterized by its free and improvised style. Therefore, the program receives a limited amount of information to start off picking a theme which will be used to determine the words that should be selected from a pool of vocabulary, either for their meaning or intent, in order to build a coherent sentence.

Ruins of the Treasure
The Ruins of the Treasure is a game developed in Unreal Engine to learn the platform and explore its blueprint system. With my background in programming and Unity, I created an adventure where players navigate ancient ruins, dodge projectiles from enemy towers, and use their own projectiles to destroy these towers. The ultimate goal is to reach a chest filled with treasure on the other side of the ruins. I developed several key blueprints for this project, including the enemyshooter, which fires at the player every 3 seconds and self-destructs when hit by the player’s projectile. Another blueprint handles UI updates for the player, changing when damage is taken and managing collision detection to ensure smooth gameplay.
